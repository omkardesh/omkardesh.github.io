Fort Torna

  • Trek Facts and Essential Information
  • Ideal Itinerary You Can Follow

How to reach: One can take his/her own vehicle and park at the base. It is safe to park the vehicle at Velhe. One can alight State Transport bus going to Velhe from Swargate (Pune). The first bus is at around 6:30 am. Please confirm once with the Swargate bus depot before deporting.

Height: 1,403 metres (4,603 ft) above sea level

Base Village: Velhe, off Nasrapur Phata on NH4 (Pune-Banglore Highway).

Distance : From Pune:60 km (approx.) | From Mumbai:200 km (approx.)

Location on the map:

bitgeeks


Trek Facts

Trek Grade: Moderate

This trek is actually easy to encounter in terms of trekking and no special equipments like rope are required. However, what makes it challenging is the height you have to climb being the highest point of Pune district.

Attraction

  • Good rock patches.
  • Menghai Devi temple.
  • Zhunzar Machi.
  • Budhla Machi.
  • View of the adjacent forts in the region.
  • Daru Kothar (ancient arms godown)

Best Season

Winters and monsoons are the best season for this trek. If you are planning in Summers, night trek is advice and it is quite popular too. You, however, need to take some basic precaution while trekking in monsoon as the route could becomea bit slippery. So, always go with a trusted used shoes meant for trekking.

Food Availability

You need to make arrangement for food as there is no one at the top to provide you the food. Alternatively, you can contact hotels at the base village which can make food arrangements for you at the top. We suggest you to try cooking yourself as that would be a really offbeat experience on the highest point in the region.

Water Availability

The is a water tank at the top which has potable water. You can aslo fill up your bottles from the base village.

Stay

There is rock flooring around the Menghai Devi temple where you can arrange your bedding and enjoy the night under millions of stars and have a wonderful experience of your life. If you are finding uncomfortable to sleep in the open, you can choose to sleep inside the temple, however, the temple normally remains crowded by fellow trekkers. As on most of the forts, you do not see a cave. *Precaution: Due to the height of the fort, the nights are extremely windy and one needs to take precaution while erecting the tents as the winds are so strong that it can blow up the tent if no weight is put inside.

Safety

  • Climbing is very safe as there are good railings at the side of the cliff at rock climbing section.
  • No ropes requied.
  • Path from the base village to the top is easy to be navigated. Can also take help from the base village.
  • For emergency, police station is located in the base village.

List of Things You Need For This Trek

  • Backpack or a small rucksack.
  • Trekking shoes. Avoid sandles for this trek.
  • Track Pant and loose shirt that would help keep ventilate your body.
  • A pair of extra cloths and inners.
  • Two 1-lit of water bottles.
  • A spare 0.5 lit bottle for making energy drink.
  • GluconD / Electrol / ORS.
  • Garbage bag to carry your garbage.
  • Torch / head-torch.
  • Personal Medical Kit.
  • Camping knife or scissors (Not required but good to keep for any trek).

Trek Itinerary

Following are the itineraries you can follow for this trek. *Note: These itineraries are purely indicative and provided for your convenience of planning the trek.
