Fort Rajgad

  • Trek Facts
  • Ideal Itinerary

Altitude: 1376 metres (4514 ft) above sea level

Base Village: Gunjavane

Time To Reach The Top: 2-2:30 hrs

Distance : From Pune:57 km (approx.) | From Mumbai:200 km (approx.)

Location on the map:

embedgooglemap.net


How to reach:

(1)Own vehicle:
From Pune, you can take NH4 (Pune-Satara Highway) and take right turn at Nasrapur Phata. Then continue on the road towards Velhe from Nasrapur Phata till Margasani village from where you need to turn left to reach Gunjavane You can follow the path marked here.

(2)Using a public transport:
You can take a bus from Swargae bus stand going directly to Gunjavane in the morning at around 7am (timings may change and hence, it is advisable to contact the bus stand). Alternatively, take any bus going to Velhe, alight at Margasani village. Thereafter one can reach Gunjawne village via Sakhar village. Another alternative option is, take any bus going to Satara and alight at Nasrapur Phata. From there you can get local transport like jeep, auto till Margasni village. You can also check if they can take you straight to Gunjavane.

Trek Facts

Trek Grade: Easy to Moderate

The trail is easy for trek yet very interesting as you encounter many terrains like flat plateau, rock patches, a bit of jungle. You need to be, however, careful about the path as it is easy to differ from the main path at the starting point. Once you get through it, then it is easy route.

Attraction

  • Padmavati Lake.
  • Rameshwar Temple.
  • Rajwada.
  • Pali Darwaja.
  • Padmavati Machi.
  • Padmavati Temple.
  • Sanjivani Machi.
  • Alu Darwaja.
  • Suvela Machi.
  • Main fort bastion (balekilla).

Best Season

Winters and monsoons are the best season for this trek. If you are planning in Summers, night trek is advice and it is quite popular too. You, however, need to take some basic precaution while trekking in monsoon as the route could become a bit slippery. So, always go with a trusted used shoes meant for trekking.

Food Availability

You need to carry your own food if you are planning to stay. However, there have been temporary arrangements made at the top by the local villagers for providing food. Alternatively, you can make food arrangements from the base.

Water Availability

There is potable water in Padmavati Lake located opposite to the Padmavati temple at the top. The water in other ponds is not potable.

Stay

About 20 to 25 persons can be easily accommodated within Padmavati Mandir. Alternatively, tourist guest houses are available on Padmavati Machi. You can carry your tents as well.

Safety

  • Climbing is easy and manageable once you get through the initial section of the trek.
  • No ropes required.
  • The base village and the trek route is well populated (not over populated). Hence, self for even solo trekkers.

List of Things You Need For This Trek

  • Backpack or a small rucksack.
  • Trekking shoes. Avoid sandles for this trek.
  • Track Pant and loose shirt that would help keep ventilate your body.
  • Wind cheater (It is quite windy at the top).
  • A pair of extra cloths and inners.
  • Two 1-lit of water bottles.
  • A spare 0.5 lit bottle for making energy drink.
  • GluconD / Electrol / ORS.
  • Garbage bag to carry your garbage.
  • Torch / head-torch.
  • Personal Medical Kit.
  • Camping knife or scissors (Not required but good to keep for any trek).

Trek Itinerary

Following are the itineraries you can follow for this trek. *Note: These itineraries are purely indicative and provided for your convenience of planning the trek.

06:00 Start trek

09:30 Start climbing the main bastion (Balekilla)

12:30 Start exploring the fort
17:00 Start descending the fort
18:30 Reach the base
by 20:00 Reach Pune

08:00 Climb down from Ballekilla
08:30 Breakfast
