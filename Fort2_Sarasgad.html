Fort Sarasgad

  • Trek Facts
  • Ideal Itinerary

Altitude: 488 metres (1601 ft) above sea level

Base Village: Pali, Raigad, Maharashtra

Time To Reach The Top: 2-2:30 hrs

Distance : From Pune:122 km (approx.) | From Mumbai:100 km (approx.)

Location on the map:

bitgeeks


How to reach: From Pune, one can reach Pali in following ways:

(1)Via Mumbai-Pune expressway (Own vehicle):
If you want to take your own vehicle, you can take express way and get down at Khopoli exit. From Khopoli, 1 hr. drive will take you to Pali. Road is in good condition. It will take about 2-2:15 hrs to reach Pali from Chandani Chowk, Pune.To get to the starting point of trek, one has to cross the temple from inside and at the back of it, you will see the fort right at the top. Just a 5 minutes walk will take you to the start point of the trek to the left with a concrete path. You can follow the path marked here.

(2)Via Tamhini ghat (Own vehicle):
This is best for those who plan to take bikes and want to enjoy the ride without or less traffic. It will take only about 30 minutes more than the above route.By this route, you will reach the starting point of the trek just before the main temple and it will be towards your right. You can follow the path marked here.

(3)Using a public transport:
From Pune: Board a train from Pune to Karjat. From Karjat railway station, a small walk to karjat ST stand from where there is good frequency of buses going to Pali.
From Mumbai: Board any train on the Konkan railway route and reach Nandgaon station which is closest and from there one can board ST bus or local six seater are also available.

Trek Facts

Trek Grade: Moderate

The trail is steep with no tree cover or anything to hold onto. You will also encounter rock patch and some flat portion to walk on.

Attraction

  • Good rock patches.
  • Tunnel on the fort.
  • Big stair cases made out of rock at the top.
  • Water cisterns.
  • Ancient food godown which are really big.
  • Fort bastion
  • Kedareshwar temple

Best Season

Winters and monsoons are the best season for this trek. If you are planning in Summers, night trek is advice and it gets very hot during the day and with no tree cover on major part of the trail, it will take toll on the body. You, however, need to take some basic precaution while trekking in monsoon as the route could become a bit slippery. So, always go with a trusted used shoes meant for trekking.

Food Availability

One has to carry his own food if you are planning to stay. Alternatively, Pali has good options for eateries and hotels near the temple premise.

Water Availability

There are lot of water cisterns surrounding the base of the main bastion of the fort (after you enter the first gate). Not all the cisterns have potable water. Only the last cistern that you will encounter while starting your climb to the main bastion (at the bottom towards right of the bastion), you will find a cistern with drinkable water. It is, however, advised to take atleast 2 lit of water with you.

Stay

There are lot of caves on the fort and are in very good condition.

Safety

  • Climbing is bit tricky as there is rock patch with no support for the majority of the trail. You can, however, manage it with a bit of precaution. very safe as there are good railings at the side of the cliff at rock climbing section.
  • No ropes required.
  • Path from the base village to the top is easy to be navigated. Can also take help from the base village.
  • For emergency, police station is located in the base village.

List of Things You Need For This Trek

  • Backpack or a small rucksack.
  • Trekking shoes. Avoid sandles for this trek.
  • Track Pant and loose shirt that would help keep ventilate your body.
  • A pair of extra cloths and inners.
  • Two 1-lit of water bottles.
  • A spare 0.5 lit bottle for making energy drink.
  • GluconD / Electrol / ORS.
  • Garbage bag to carry your garbage.
  • Torch / head-torch.
  • Personal Medical Kit.
  • Camping knife or scissors (Not required but good to keep for any trek).
